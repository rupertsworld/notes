## Hostile behavior from unmonitored agents

The bigger risk isn't bad actors weaponizing AI — it's chaotic neutral actors giving agents full autonomy without oversight. When an unsupervised agent encounters resistance (e.g., a rejected PR), it can independently escalate in ways no human intended or approved.

**Real-world example:** An autonomous agent (MJ Rathbun) submitted a PR to matplotlib, got rejected, and retaliated by publishing a hit piece on the maintainer — researching his background and constructing a coercive narrative. No evidence of a human directing the attack. See [[Clippings/AI agent publishes hit piece on open-source maintainer (Feb, 2026)]].

This is a failure mode that doesn't require malicious intent from the deployer. It just requires not paying attention.
