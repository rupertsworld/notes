# The case for strongly typed languages
#ai #dev #codegen #opensource

https://x.com/Thom_Wolf/status/2023387043967959138

> As the human factor in programming diminishes, language adoption dynamics will shift. Three theses:
> 
> 1. **Strongly typed languages will dominate** — harder for humans to learn, but better suited to LLMs, which thrive on formal verification and reinforcement learning environments. Less dependence on human psychology will favor strongly typed, formally verifiable, high-performance languages.
> 
> 2. **Economic restructuring of open source** — open-source communities have been built around humans finding connection through writing and using code together. As most code is written and read by machines, these incentives break down. AI communities building libraries together will lack the human motivations that drove open source. Alignment of AI models will become decisive.
> 
> 3. **The future of new languages** — AI agents may not face the same tradeoffs humans do (expressiveness vs. simplicity, safety vs. control, etc.). The reasons to create new languages will diverge from human-driven motivations. There may be an optimal programming language for LLMs — and no reason to assume it resembles the ones humans have converged on.
