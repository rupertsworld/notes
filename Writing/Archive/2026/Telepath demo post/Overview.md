## Audience

People building tech products (at incumbents or startups) - specifically designers, product folks, developers, founders, investors.

**What they think:**
- Either uncertain about where AI-enabled computing is headed
- Or pursuing directions that are misguided: adding AI features to existing paradigms, iterating on chat interfaces

**What they value:**
- Concrete visions that help orient product decisions
- Understanding where the industry is heading
- Preparing for disruption (particularly: the death of apps)

**How we add value:**
- Provide a concrete, well-reasoned vision of what computing becomes with LLMs
- Give them a north star to orient their product decisions around
- Help them understand the fundamental shift, not just incremental changes
- Warn app developers of existential disruption

**Our credibility comes from:**
- Background and demo track record (Google Creative Lab, Adept)
- Depth of thought (Territory partnership, research process, user interviews)
- Specificity and concreteness of the vision
- Production value/coolness

## Purpose

Not "look at our cool demo" but "if you're building products and unsure where this is all going, here's a coherent vision that can help you think about the future of computing."

The key message has two parts:
1. **Apps are going away** - the app paradigm is ending, this is existential disruption
2. **Here's what the GUI looks like instead** - concrete vision of the interface paradigm that replaces apps

If you're building by adding AI to existing paradigms or iterating on chat, you're missing the fundamental shift. Here's what computing looks like when you rebuild it from scratch.
