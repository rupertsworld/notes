# AGENTS.md

## Project Overview

This is a **design-focused blog post** about Telepath's AI-first personal computer vision demo, presented at Betaworks Demo Day in November.

## Goal

Write an article that presents a concrete, north-star vision for where personal computing is headed with AI. The core thesis has two parts:

1. **Apps are going away** — the traditional app model doesn't make sense in an AI-native world
2. **Here's what replaces them** — a vision of dynamic, generative interfaces that compose fluidly based on context

This is NOT a business pitch or product announcement. It's a design piece showing what's possible when you rethink the GUI from first principles with AI at the core.

## Target Audience

**Primary:** Designers
**Secondary:** Product folks, developers, founders, investors

Keep technical depth appropriate for designers — focus on interaction patterns, user experience, and visual thinking rather than implementation details.

## Demo Narrative

The demo shows a knowledge worker researching a topic in real-time, with the AI system:

- Generating contextual interfaces on the fly (no apps)
- Understanding user priorities and context
- Adapting to different input modes (voice, visual)
- Presenting information as fluid, morphable cards in 3D space

## Three Core Capabilities

The article is structured around three capabilities that form the demo's story:

1. **Dynamic Interfaces / Generative UI** — Interfaces synthesized on demand, not pre-built apps
2. **Sensemaking** — The system builds understanding of the user over time
3. **Situational Awareness** — Context-aware input/output that adapts to the moment
