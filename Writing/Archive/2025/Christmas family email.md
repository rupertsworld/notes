# Christmas email

Hello friends & family,

As we come to the close of another year, I wanted to take the opportunity to write and check in with you all, and give you an update on our 2025.

I’m writing this from Melbourne, where we just landed and will be spending Christmas this year. My mum Kay was a Melbourner, and I have fond memories as a kid of driving down the Hume and waking up to spend Christmas morning with my grandparents, uncle and cousins (usually to hail or something – hey, a white christmas!). It's been some time since my last trip down, and since we became a little family this will be our first Christmas outside Sydney. But with a good chunk of Izzi's family away for the holidays, now seemed the perfect opportunity to hang out with the Stammerses. We're really looking forward to it.

I read the Economist's year in review on the flight over, and it's jaw-dropping how many seismic events happened on our planet over the past year. The most recent of which was of course the shooting in Bondi, at the location where we held Mina's 4th birthday just a few months before. We've had many people in our lives affected by this, and our hearts open for them and the many Jewish Sydneysiders feeling unsafe in our emerald city. And likewise, to our friends who are rebuilding or establishing themselves abroad after the devastating LA fires at the start of the year.

We're still in San Francisco, but have spent *almost* as many months in Sydney as the Bay, which—while being somewhat insane logistics-wise, in keeping with our general vibe—has been wonderful.

The main reason is because Izzi's band is back together and have been touring & releasing music! They were touring all around Australia through Aussie winter to sold out crowds, and last month celebrated 10 years of *Blue Planet Eyes* with an exhibition in Sydney. They released a live record from the tour, which you can find here.

Mina meanwhile is turning into the consummate traveler, racking up some decent international miles. For a while, we were worried that our back & forth would be a huge disruption to her life and sense of home. To the contrary, Mina now feels like she's part of a big international community. When I once asked her whether she'd rather have her home in San Francisco or Sydney she said proudly, "I like living in both." She has besties either side of the Pacific – how cool is that?? 

She's a boistrous four-year old now. Her hobbies include pretending to be a teacher and bossing people around (not sure where she gets that from...), walking behind the counter at stores and helping them serve customers, wrestling her parents, and doing some fabulous drawings. And after some Aussie pool lessons she just learned how to keep herself swimming without any floaties. She continues to be intensely curious and personable.

As for me, I finally found two co-founders in September after almost a year going on my own in startup world. They're both friends & former colleagues from my time at Mozilla. We gave a demo of our work in progress last month which you can watch here. I've also been spending a bunch of time in nature, often with Mina by my side, learning about and documenting the diversity of species between the US & Australia (did you know redwoods create their own rain?). Mina and I had our first night camping together which she loved.

Anyway that's the latest from us. And before we wrap, a big shout to old man Tristan Parry who recently celebrated his 70th in Sydney.

Wishing you all a Merry Christmas and a happy holidays!

Love,
Rupert, Mina & Izzi
