

# Hello, Telepath.

Today, I'm excited to share that Unternet is joining forces with [Telepath](https://telepath.com), as my former Mozilla colleagues Steven, Josh, and I unite as co-founders to build a fundamentally new kind of computer.

##  My year of Unternet

Over the past year, Unternet has been my laboratory for exploring how AI can transform the relationship between users and their software. Working primarily solo with occasional contractor support, I've been deep in R&D on a core question: what if the browser—that "user agent" we all rely on—actually acted as a true _agent_ on your behalf?

This research phase taught me what's possible, what's hard, and where the real leverage points lie. The crucial insight: to truly reimagine how humans interact with computers, we need to go beyond the browser layer and rethink the computer itself from the ground up, at the operating system level.

## Meeting my new co-founders

Just before I left Mozilla, I had one week of collaboration with Stephen and Josh that stands out as the best working week of my life. We challenged each other, built on each other's ideas, and moved with a velocity I hadn't experienced before or since. I've wanted to work with them again ever since.

A few months ago, Stephen reached out. He was leaving Mozilla to start Telepath, focused on the future of computing. His vision—rethinking fundamental computing metaphors, building AI as a native part of the system, creating computers that can make sense of our digital lives—aligned remarkably with what I'd been uncovering at Unternet.

Over the following months, it became clear that what we each understood independently became something more powerful in combination.
