## What we built at Unternet

Over the past year, Unternet has been my laboratory for exploring fundamental questions about the future of the web. Working primarily as a solo founder, with a bit of help from some talented friends, I've been deep in R&D on what happens when the *user agent* actually acts as an agent on the user's behalf. And, simultaneously, what it looks like to have machine intelligence act on the richness of the graphical web, not just text chat. 

I’ve been deep in R&D on how this new fundamental technology can transform the relationship between users and the internet.

The core insight driving this work: what if the browser—that “user agent” we all rely on—actually acted as a true *agent* on your behalf? What if applications could be represented more graphically and richly, with language models orchestrating complex interactions between them?

This research phase has been invaluable. I’ve learned what’s possible, what’s hard, and where the real leverage points lie. And I’ve arrived at a crucial realization: to truly reimagine how humans interact with computers, we need to think beyond the browser layer. We need to rethink the computer itself from the ground up.

## The best week at Mozilla

Just before I left Mozilla, I had one week of collaboration with Steven and Josh that stands out as the best working week of my life. The chemistry was undeniable—we challenged each other, built on each other’s ideas, and moved with a velocity I hadn’t experienced before or since. I always wanted to work with them again, and they felt the same way.

A few months ago, Steven reached out. He was leaving Mozilla to start something new, focused on the future of computing. His vision was still forming, but the core principles he outlined—how computing should work, what’s broken, what’s possible—aligned remarkably with what I’d been discovering through my work at Unternet.

Over the following months, our conversations deepened. It became clear that what we each understood independently became something more powerful in combination. The research I’d done, the insights they brought, the hard-earned lessons from our respective journeys—it all pointed in the same direction.

## What's next

At Telepath, we’re building the computing platform we believe should exist. One that treats AI not as a chatbot bolted onto existing systems, but as a fundamental reimagining of how computers serve human needs.

The work we did at Unternet remains valuable—those insights about AI-mediated interaction, about orchestrating applications through natural language, about what users actually need from their software. But they need a different foundation to reach their full potential.

I’m grateful for the Unternet journey and excited to bring everything I learned into this next phase. Building alongside Steven and Josh, we have the chance to create something truly transformative.
